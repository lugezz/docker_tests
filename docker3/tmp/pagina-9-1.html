Description

The City of Moorhead is accepting applications for a GIS Manager in the Engineering Department. This professional technical position performs difficult work managing the development, coordination and maintenance of the City's computer-based Geographic Information System, and related work as required. Provides leadership, consultation, and guidance for City Departments in the development and use of GIS. This position exercises general supervision over assigned staff.

ESSENTIAL FUNCTIONS

  • Collaborates with all City departments to determine their specific needs and develops GIS applications to meet those needs by more effectively managing data and more efficiently allocating resources.
  • Manages, supervises and quality checks the maintenance and update of the City's land base and cadastral layers in the GIS database.
  • Manages, supervises and quality checks the maintenance and update of the City's utility and transportation infrastructure layers in the GIS database.
  • Assigns all new addressing in the City, and reviews proposals for compliance with address standards. Performs updates to the City site addresses in the Clay County tax database.
  • Maintenance of GIS master address points and street centerline data for compliance with Minnesota Next Generation 911 Data Standards.
  • Notification of addressing to RRRDC 911 dispatch center contact, and all other interested parties.
  • Coordinates, trains and supervises the activities of GIS technical staff.
  • Manages consultant contracts for making improvements to and creating new applications for the City GIS.
  • Represents Moorhead in the Metro region aerial imagery and LiDAR collection project that occurs every three years.
  • Administers and maintains the City's Enterprise GIS internet and intranet map services and websites.
  • Reviews existing and new City database information for quality and relevance, and develops plans for integration with the City’s GIS.
  • Trains City staff in use of GIS software, processes and tools.
  • Troubleshoots GIS applications and database issues.
  • Generates ad-hoc and custom maps as requested by City staff and the general public.
  • Performs mapping and documentation of City's flood mitigation projects to maintain the City's emergency flood plan and FEMA accredited LOMC areas in GIS.
  • Coordinates licensing of GIS software in cooperation with the Information Services Department.
  • Coordinates and communicates with other public entities on regional planning and GIS standards.
  • Serves as City representative in various public and private forums.
  • Performs other duties as requested.
  • Attendance at off-site courses/trainings/seminars may be required.
Supervisory Position: The GIS Manager performs or effectively recommends the hiring, transfer, suspension, promotion, discharge, assignment, reward, discipline, direction of work, and adjustment of grievances of subordinate personnel.
 

MINIMUM REQUIREMENTS
To perform the GIS Manager position successfully, an individual must be able to perform each essential function satisfactorily. The requirements listed below are representative of the formal education, experience and training required.
 

Education and Experience

  • Bachelor's degree in a geospatial emphasis, or related field
  • Five years experience in municipal Geographic Information Systems, or related field; Management of GIS preferred
  • Supervision experience preferred

Training, Certificates, and Licenses

  • GIS Professional Certification, preferred but not required.
  • ESRI ArcGIS Desktop software suite
  • ESRI ArcGIS Enterprise database management software
  • Microsoft SQL database management software
  • Valid driver's license


KNOWLEDGE, SKILLS, and ABILITIES
The GIS Manager requires comprehensive knowledge of ESRI GIS products and software; comprehensive knowledge in the development and implementation of GIS software in a networked environment; thorough knowledge of GIS, data formats; general knowledge of other related GIS products and programs; general knowledge of surveying and civil engineering principles, procedures and techniques; general knowledge of engineering maps and records; general knowledge of city, state and federal policies, laws and regulations; general skill operating necessary tools and equipment such as GPS, spreadsheet, database, GIS and CAD software and standard drafting tools; ability to develop and maintain various geographic and attribute databases in various formats using GIS industry standard software and hardware; ability to be creative and to perform independent decision-making and evaluations; ability to solve problems without defined methods or procedures; skills in project management and project planning ability to interpret operating, maintenance, technical and procedure manuals, engineering plans and specifications; ability to communicate effectively, both orally and in writing; ability to understand and carry out written and oral instructions; ability to use independent judgment to provide supervision and leadership to department personnel; ability to manage budgets; ability to establish and maintain effective working relationships with associates, supervisors, other departments, vendors and the general public.

PHYSICAL REQUIREMENTS and WORKING CONDITIONS
This work requires the occasional exertion of up to 25 pounds of force; work regularly requires sitting and occasionally requires standing, walking, speaking or hearing, using hands to finger, handle or feel, reaching with hands and arms, lifting and repetitive motions; work has standard vision requirements; vocal communication is required for expressing or exchanging ideas by means of the spoken word; hearing is required to perceive information at normal spoken word levels; work requires preparing and analyzing written or computer data, using of measuring devices, operating machines, operating motor vehicles or equipment and observing general surroundings and activities; ; work is generally in a moderately noisy location (e.g. business office, light traffic).
